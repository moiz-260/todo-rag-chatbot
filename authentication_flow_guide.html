The Authentication Journey

A simple guide to how your Next.js + MongoDB authentication system works.

01. The Secret Connection (lib/mongodb.ts)

Before any data can be saved, we need a map to the storage room (MongoDB). We use "Lazy Connection"â€”the app only connects when it actually needs to perform a task. This keeps the app fast and prevents random crashes if the database is busy.

02. The Vault Door (models/User.ts)

This defines the rules of who can enter. Most importantly, it handles Password Hashing. When you enter "123456", we use bcrypt to turn it into a long string like "$2a$10$X...". This ensures no one can ever see your real password, even in the database.

03. Creating the Account (api/auth/signup)

When you click "Sign Up", the "Route Handler" acts as the library clerk. It:

  • Validates your inputs (Is the email valid? Is the name empty?).
  • Checks if you already have an account.
  • Saves your info securely.
  • Hands you a JWT (JSON Web Token).
The Golden Ticket (JWT): Think of this as a wristband at a concert. You show your ID once at the door, get the wristband, and then you can go in and out of the VIP areas just by showing the wristband.

04. The Bouncer (api/auth/signin)

When you come back to log in, the bouncer compares the password you typed with the hashed version in the vault. If they match, you get a fresh Golden Ticket!

The Turbopack Challenge

We switched from Turbopack back to Webpack (using the --webpack flag). Turbopack is new and fast, but it sometimes gets confused by complex libraries like Mongoose. Webpack is the "Grandmaster" that knows how to handle everything perfectly.
